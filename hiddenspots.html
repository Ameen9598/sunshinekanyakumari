Kaalikesam Waterfallst
Kaalikesam Waterfalls in Kanyakumari is a hidden gem, surrounded by lush forests and cascading from a height of 100 feet. A serene spot for nature lovers and trekkers, it offers a peaceful escape with stunning views. Close to the Kaalikesam Temple, the site combines natural beauty and spiritual significance, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Udayagiri Fort
Udayagiri Fort, located near Padmanabhapuram in Kanyakumari district, is a historic 17th-century fort built by the rulers of Travancore. Surrounded by lush greenery, the fort once served as a key military base and houses the tomb of Dutch commander Eustachius De Lannoy. Today, it's a serene spot combining history, nature, and heritage. A must-visit for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

Pechparai Reservoir
Pechiparai Reservoir, nestled in the foothills of the Western Ghats in Kanyakumari district, is a scenic water body surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills. Built during the British era, it serves both irrigation and drinking water needs. The tranquil setting and lush greenery make it a perfect spot for nature lovers and picnickers. Ideal for a peaceful getaway amid nature.

Muttom Beach
Muttom Beach, located near Nagercoil in Kanyakumari district, is known for its rocky shoreline, dramatic cliffs, and stunning sunsets. Less crowded and beautifully serene, it's a perfect spot for peaceful walks and photography. The nearby lighthouse adds to its charm, making it a hidden gem for travelers seeking natural beauty.

Thirparappu Waterfalls
Thirparappu Waterfalls, located about 55 km from Kanyakumari, is a stunning man-made waterfall formed by the Kodayar River. Cascading from a height of nearly 50 feet, it's a popular spot for family outings and safe bathing. The surrounding greenery and nearby ancient Shiva temple enhance its scenic and spiritual appeal. A perfect blend of nature and culture.

Vattakottai
Vattakottai Fort, meaning “Circular Fort,” is a seaside fort near Kanyakumari built in the 18th century by the Travancore kingdom. Made of granite, it offers panoramic views of the sea on one side and the Western Ghats on the other. The peaceful surroundings and coastal breeze make it a perfect historical and picnic spot. Ideal for history lovers and photographers.

Nagaraja Temple
Nagaraja Temple, located in Nagercoil, is a unique and ancient temple dedicated to Nagaraja, the Serpent King. Known for its sacred snake worship, the temple features a sand-filled sanctum and serene atmosphere. Devotees visit for blessings related to fertility, health, and protection from serpent-related doshas. A spiritual destination rich in tradition and devotion.

Chothavillai Beach
Chothavilai Beach, located about 10 km from Kanyakumari, is one of the longest and cleanest beaches in the region. Known for its shallow waters and gentle waves, it's ideal for swimming and family outings. The peaceful ambiance and scenic beauty make it a perfect spot for relaxation and sunset views. A hidden gem away from the usual crowds.

Shri Bhagavathi Amman Temple
Shri Bhagavathi Amman Temple, located in Kanyakumari, is a revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Bhagavathi, an incarnation of Parvati. The temple is believed to have been built over 1,000 years ago and holds immense spiritual significance, especially during the annual Chaitra festival. The serene surroundings and historical architecture make it a must-visit for pilgrims and history enthusiasts alike.

Sanguthurai Beach
Sanguthurai Beach, located near Nagercoil, offers a serene escape with its golden sands and gentle waves. This less-crowded beach is perfect for a peaceful stroll, sunset views, and a relaxing day by the sea. Surrounded by natural beauty and a tranquil atmosphere, it’s an ideal spot for those looking to enjoy the beach without the usual hustle and bustle.

Padmanabhapuram Palace
Padmanabhapuram Palace, located about 15 km from Kanyakumari, is a stunning example of traditional Kerala architecture. Built in the 16th century, this wooden palace served as the residence of the Travancore kings. With its intricate carvings, royal murals, and beautiful courtyards, the palace offers a glimpse into the grandeur and cultural heritage of the Travancore dynasty.

Tsunami Memorial Park
Tsunami Memorial Park, located in Kanyakumari, commemorates the victims of the devastating 2004 tsunami. The park features a serene memorial with inscriptions and statues, offering a place for reflection and remembrance. Set against a picturesque backdrop of the sea, it serves as a peaceful and poignant reminder of the resilience of the local community.

Suchindram Shree Thanumalayan Swamy Temple
Suchindram Shri Thanumalayan Swamy Temple, located near Kanyakumari, is a revered temple dedicated to the trinity of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Known for its unique architectural style, the temple boasts intricate sculptures, a massive 22-foot tall statue of Thanumalayan, and the famous musical pillars. A must-visit for spiritual seekers and lovers of ancient architecture.
